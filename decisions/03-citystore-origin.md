## Redefine From CityShop to CityStore

### Context

After discontinuing CityShop, the search for a stronger customer-focused problem continued.

Several real-world experiences revealed a recurring issue.

---

### Incident 1: Clothing Shopping

While purchasing clothes, it was difficult to determine:

- Which store offered the best price
- Whether a specific product was available
- Whether visiting the store would be worthwhile

The only available solution was:

1. Visit one store
2. Ask for products
3. Check prices
4. Visit another store
5. Compare manually

This process was time-consuming and inefficient.

---

### Incident 2: Restaurant Pricing

While planning a birthday celebration, restaurant menus were checked online before visiting.

The menu available online contained outdated pricing.

Upon arriving at the restaurant, a newer menu with significantly higher prices was presented.

The customer had no reliable way to verify current pricing before visiting.

---

### Key Observation

The larger problem was not delivery.

The larger problem was information discovery.

Customers frequently struggle to answer:

- Which store has the best price?
- Which store currently has stock?
- Which salon provides a specific service?
- Which restaurant fits my budget?
- Which businesses exist in my city?
- Which business should I visit?

---

### Decision

Stop focusing on delivery as the primary product value.

Instead, build a platform that makes local businesses discoverable and transparent.

The new product direction becomes:

# CityStore

> Your City. Every Store. One App.


### CityStore Vision

Every local business should have an online presence.

Customers should be able to:

- Discover businesses
- Browse catalogs
- Compare prices
- View products
- View services
- Check availability
- Book appointments
- Contact business owners

without physically visiting multiple locations.

no matter in which city you are, you have whole city in your mobile phone inside the **CityStore**.

---

### Optional Commerce

Ordering is no longer the core offering.

However, businesses may optionally enable:

- Online ordering
- Home delivery
- Custom delivery conditions
- Minimum order requirements
- Free delivery thresholds

Delivery responsibility remains with the business itself.

---

### Expected Benefits

#### Customers

- Better decision making
- Easier comparison
- Current information
- Less wasted time

#### Business Owners

- Increased visibility
- Digital presence
- Customer acquisition
- Direct communication channels

---

### Outcome

CityStore becomes the new primary product direction.
