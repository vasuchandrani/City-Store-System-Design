## Stop Development of CityShop


### Context

After defining the product requirements and creating the initial prototype for CityShop, the overall business model was reviewed.

The platform was designed around three stakeholders:

- Customers
- Shopkeepers
- Delivery Partners

The planned revenue model was:

### Shopkeeper Revenue

Charge local shopkeepers a subscription fee to register and operate their shops on the platform.

### Customer Revenue

Charge customers delivery fees for each order placed through the platform.

The platform's value proposition was built around:

- Supporting local businesses
- Online ordering
- Quick-commerce style delivery

---

### Problem Identified

During validation of the business model, a critical question emerged:

> Why would customers choose CityShop?

Existing platforms already provide:

- Massive product catalogs
- Strong brand trust
- Fast delivery
- Established logistics networks
- Free delivery above minimum order thresholds

Examples include:

- Blinkit
- Zepto
- Swiggy Instamart
- BigBasket
- Amazon
- Flipkart

Many of these platforms have significantly larger inventories, stronger logistics infrastructure, and aggressive pricing strategies.

As a result, CityShop struggled to offer a sufficiently compelling reason for customers to switch.

---

### Business Dependency Problem

The platform depended on two groups:

### Customers

Customers must actively use the platform.

### Shopkeepers

Shopkeepers must pay for subscriptions.

However:

- Without customers, shopkeepers have no incentive to subscribe.
- Without shopkeepers, customers have no reason to use the platform.

This creates a classic marketplace chicken-and-egg problem.

---

### Core Observation

Supporting local businesses is a meaningful mission.

However, a mission alone is not enough.

The product must solve a strong customer problem.

At this stage, CityShop solved a problem for shopkeepers more effectively than it solved a problem for customers.

The customer value proposition was not sufficiently differentiated from existing commerce platforms.

---

### Decision

Stop further development of CityShop.

until a stronger customer-centric value proposition is identified.

---

###  Learned

A product must first solve a clear customer problem.

Good intentions do not guarantee product-market fit.

Marketplace businesses require strong incentives for both supply and demand.
